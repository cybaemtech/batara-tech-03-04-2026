1. Aerospace
This sector focuses on high-precision engineering and manufacturing readiness for aircraft components and major assemblies. Key activities include conducting detailed stress analysis to ensure structural integrity and managing the complex transition from Engineering Bill of Materials (EBOM) to Manufacturing Bill of Materials (MBOM). The goal is to help manufacturers navigate high backlogs by improving the efficiency of their design-to-production cycles and ensuring full compliance with material standards to reduce risk.
2. Automotive
This field involves the comprehensive design and development of vehicle modules and components for diverse transport needs. Work often extends beyond standard cars to include bus and coach manufacturing, as well as specialized trailers and trucks. Engineering teams in this space focus on creating new modules that gain market praise and using FTA (Formal Technical Assessment) in CAD to enhance the accuracy and quality of products before they hit the assembly line.
3. Industrial Machinery
This industry revolves around creating complex equipment and tools tailored for specific manufacturing environments. It requires deep domain expertise to solve intricate vertical challenges and drive gains in operational efficiency. Projects in this area often involve designing hardware that can handle the distinct nuances of various industrial segments with precision.
4. Industrial Automation
This sector emphasizes the modernization of production environments through smart technology, such as developing PLC (Programmable Logic Controller) solutions for electronics and monitoring systems. It involves revamping aging industrial assets (brownfield plants) to bring them up to modern standards. By integrating digital tools, facilities can improve their ROI through better management of energy, HVAC, and safety systems.
5. Electro-Mechanical
While not listed as a standalone category in the industry list, the sources highlight work in this area through the development of monitoring systems and specialized electronics hardware. This field bridges the gap between mechanical design and electronic control, focusing on the creation of integrated systems that require both structural design and sophisticated programming to function effectively.
6. Heavy Machinery
This area focuses on the structural design and engineering of robust machinery used in demanding environments, such as off-highway and construction sites. Projects often involve navigating challenging timelines and staff needs to deliver high-quality structural layouts for massive equipment. It also covers the design of skid-mounted plants and other heavy, modular industrial assets.
